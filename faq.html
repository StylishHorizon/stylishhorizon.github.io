Why 768x576 (or 640x480) resolution?

For the Geeks: MiniDV uses non-square pixels. On modern screens, raw footage looks distorted.

We mathematically correct the geometry to Square Pixels based on the tape origin:
• PAL (Europe): Corrected to 768x576.
• NTSC (US/Japan): Corrected to 640x480.

This ensures faces look natural and circles are actually round, regardless of where the tape was filmed.

Why is the file so huge?

We deliver in ProRes LT. It captures every grain of film without compression artifacts. A 60-minute tape can be 20-30GB.

In the "Signature" tier, we also give you a smaller "Mobile Ready" file for WhatsApp.

Why are there black bars on the sides?

Old video is square (4:3). Modern TVs are wide (16:9).

The "Signature" Standard: We use a technique called "Cinematic Fill" (like Netflix documentaries). We fill the side bars with a blurred, darkened extension of the video. It looks premium and uses the full screen without cutting the image.

Can you crop to fill? Yes, we can zoom in to remove the bars, but you will lose about 25% of the image (top and bottom). Heads and feet are often cut off. We advise against it.
